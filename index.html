Which of the Following is Not a Type of Beer?

Dortmunder

A German full-bodied, moderately hopped lager that tends to be slightly more alcoholic than Helles.

Barley Wine

A strongly malted, highly hopped ale with very high alcohol content. They tend to be sweet and bitter and benefit from aging

Saisons

A typically fruity and spicy Belgian farmhouse ale, which tends to be semi-dry and a cloudy golden color.

Fernweh

Hooray, this indeed isn't a beer. It's a German word for an urge to be far from home and experience something new.

Rauchbier

A German lager whose malts are dried over a fire, imparting a strong smoky flavor to the beer.

A Hop is a ....

Seed

Nope, but barley (sometimes replaced by wheat, rye, or oat) is the seed of the barley plant and is malted then used in beer to provide sugars for the yeast to ferment and imparts sweet and grainy flavors.

Flower

Yup. Hops are the cone shaped flowers that give beer a bitterness to balance the malty sweetness.

Root

Roots are not commonly used in the brewing process, but many roots like ginger and wild rose roots have been used by microbreweries to give their beers unique flavors.

Fungus

Hops aren't a fungus, but yeast is a single-celled fungus responsible for converting the malt sugars into alcohol.

Grain

Hops are not a grain, but many grains are used in beer including barley, wheat, rye, oats, rice, corn, and sorghum.
